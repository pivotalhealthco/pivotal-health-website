Start where you actually are

Not where you used to be, or wish you were. Injured, deconditioned, overwhelmed, out of rhythm — that's the doorway, not a disqualification. Healing and building are the same road, walked at the right pace.

Consistency before intensity

Show up and do the basics well before adding more. Simple, done consistently, beats perfect done occasionally — every time. Programs don't build strength. Repeated weeks do.

03

Keep it simple and repeatable

The plan you can actually repeat is the only plan that works. Simplicity is not a compromise — it's wisdom. We find the next achievable step, execute it well, and build momentum that doesn't require perfect conditions.

My Story

I'm Andy, founder of Pivotal Health Co. I'm a combat veteran, and injuries from that deployment left me in enough pain that a doctor told me I'd never lift the way I used to. Today I'm still competing in powerlifting while managing health complications in real time. The gap between what I was told and what I'm still doing is what this business is built on.

It didn't start there. I spent most of high school overweight and out of shape — until one summer changed everything: fifty pounds lighter, and a real sense of what my body could actually do. That summer got me into wrestling, and a few years later, into the Army.

Years after leaving the service, life caught back up with me — stress, old habits, and time away from training brought me back up to 180 pounds and out of shape. This time, instead of chasing a dramatic comeback, I started small: one habit, one workout, one week at a time. The rebuild back down to the 140s, lean and strong, is the same approach I use with every client. Not because it's exciting — because it's the only thing that lasts.

I'm not coaching from a chapter I closed years ago. I'm managing injuries and health complications right now — still training, still competing, still lean. Everything I ask a client to do, I'm doing this week.

If you're a lifter who's been told to slow down, scale back, or hang it up — or you're carrying a job, a family, old injuries, and not much spare time — this was built for you.

— Andy, Founder

Common Questions

What you're probably wondering.

What if I have injuries or am dealing with a health condition?

That's most of my clients — and me. I coach around injuries and health complications every day, in my own training and my clients'. I'm not a doctor and I don't replace one — no diagnosis, no treatment, and I work alongside whatever your providers have cleared. What I do is build training and nutrition that respect your situation instead of ignoring it.

I'm a powerlifter — is this just general fitness coaching?

No. Powerlifting is the specialization. Programming is built around the squat, bench, and deadlift, your meet timeline, and whatever your body is currently arguing about. If you're chasing a total while managing a body that's pushing back, this is the lane.

How much time does this actually require each week?

Your training sessions, plus about ten minutes for check-ins. The plan is built around your schedule — most of my clients have jobs, families, and old injuries. If a plan needs a perfect week to work, it isn't a plan.

What makes this different from just following a program online?

A program doesn't watch how you move, adjust when your shoulder flares up, or answer the question you have at 9pm. This does. Your plan changes as your body and your life change, and someone is accountable for it working. Templates are cheap. Adjustments are the value.

What happens after I apply?

I read every application and reply within a couple of days. If it looks like a fit, we talk — no pressure, no script — about where you are and what you're trying to keep doing. If I'm not the right coach for what you need, I'll tell you that too.
