How I Coach

The goal isn't the hardest plan. It's the right one.

Most people don't need more information. They need a plan they can actually follow, and someone to hold them to it.

Principle 01

Consistency before intensity

Show up and do the basics well before adding more. Simple, done consistently, beats perfect done occasionally — every time.

Principle 02

Start where you actually are

Not where you used to be, or wish you were. Injured, deconditioned, overwhelmed, out of rhythm — that's the doorway, not a problem. We build from there.

Principle 03

Keep it simple and repeatable

Small habits that compound beat the perfect plan you can't sustain. We find the next achievable step, execute it well, and build momentum you can keep.

My Story

I'm Andy, founder of Pivotal Health Co. I believe strength and health aren't reserved for the young, the genetically gifted, or people with all the time in the world.

I spent most of high school overweight and out of shape — until one summer changed everything: fifty pounds lighter, and a real sense of what my body could actually do. That summer got me into wrestling, and a few years later, into the Army, where injuries from a combat deployment left me in enough pain that a doctor told me I'd never lift the way I used to.

Years after leaving the service, life caught back up with me. Stress, old habits, and time away from training brought me back up to 180 pounds and out of shape. This time, instead of chasing a dramatic comeback, I started small — one habit, one workout, one week at a time. The rebuild back down to the 140s, lean and strong, is the same approach I use with every client. Not because it's exciting, but because it's the only thing that lasts.

Most of the people I work with have jobs, families, old injuries, and not much spare time — and that's exactly who this is built for. I've been overweight, and I've been hurt badly enough to be told I'd never lift again, and I've rebuilt from both. I do this because I believe people are capable of more than they think, and I want to help them find that out.

— Andy, Founder
